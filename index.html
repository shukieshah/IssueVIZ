IssueVIZ leverages data-driven visualizations to help you analyze trending presidential election issues and key words during election years.

Stay Informed

IssueViz was built to understand what topics were of importance regarding the American presidential elections, to be able to better see the issues that are at the forefront of our nation and in the minds of voters. Whether you are a first-time voter, a long-time voter, or merely an observer of American politics, we hope you use this tool to visualize key election topics, understand how different issues trend over time, and maybe even start to form your own ideas about the kinds of issues that are important to you.

How It Works


All of the data for IssueVIZ is sourced from reddit.com. To construct the visualizations, we first retrieved a general list of issues/topics for each presidential election, taken from online political forums. These lists were aggregated to form a total of 40 overarching election issues/topics spanning the last four presidential election cycles. Then, we constructed a mapping from each overarching issue to the associated “keywords” that are relevant to the category:



For each keyword, we mined the frequency of unique Reddit comments containing that keyword. The Reddit comments analyzed were those posted from January 1st through December 31st of each US presidential election year since 2005. To account for high comment variance, we limited our analysis to popular political subreddits, specifically r/politics, r/politicaldiscussion, r/neutralpolitics, and r/ask_politics. To calculate the popularity of a particular issue for a specific election year, we simply added all of the frequency counts associated with the relevant keywords during the given year. For further details, please refer to our GitHub project repository.

Using this data, you can visualize the top issues or key words (ranked based on comment frequency) for each election year. Alternatively, you can analyze the overarching trends over time.

Histogram




Histogram displays the key election topics for the year in a bar chart format, making it easier for viewers to clearly see the order of topics in frequency, as well as the number of times the topic was referenced.




Bubble View breaks down topics into "key words", where a topic may be associated with more than one word. This provides the viewer with a more specific breakdown of topics, by allowing them to hover on different words and see its frequency.

Bubble View

Word Cloud




Word Cloud provides an interesting visualization for viewers to look at topics. They can associate the size of the word with the relative frequency, providing stark contrast between high-frequency and low-frequency topics.




Trends displays all the top topics at once, showing its evolution over a time. Here, viewers can see which topics became more or less important throughout the years, and which ones stayed the same. They can use the selection tool to pick and choose which topics to look at.
